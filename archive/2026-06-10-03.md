## Key decisions

- **es-atlas as communities source**: province GeoJSON edges don't share exact coordinates
  between adjacent features, so any dissolve approach leaves visible seam artifacts.
  es-atlas provides purpose-built community geometries — no dissolve, no artifacts.
  URL: `https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/es-atlas@0.6.0/es/autonomous_regions.json`
  (TopoJSON, readable by fiona/geopandas with `driver="TopoJSON"`)

- **`--communities` flag**: when iterating on communities.svg only, avoids downloading
  the province GeoJSON (~5 MB) and re-running provinces.svg generation.
  Example: `python scripts/generate_svg.py --communities`

- **stroke:none + border overlay**: community fill paths have no stroke at all;
  a `<g id="community-borders">` group drawn on top provides the visible outlines.
  This makes province artifacts structurally impossible regardless of data source quality.

- **What was tried and failed**: area-based sliver filtering, buffer-snap
  (`buffer(SNAP).union.buffer(-SNAP)`), `set_precision` grid snapping — all reduced but
  did not eliminate artifacts because the root cause was gaps that appeared as interior
  rings in the dissolved polygon, not as separate tiny polygons.
